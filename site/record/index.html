Closing-Line Scoreboard

Did our flagged price hold to post?

Before every race we publish a “play at X or better” price. In a pari-mutuel pool your payout is set by the final odds no matter when you bet, so the only price that counts is the close. After the race we check whether the closing tote odds actually reached the price we asked for. If they did, the value we flagged was real and takeable; if the horse closed shorter, it was a pass — not a bet you missed by timing.

ML→close = to-1 points the price moved from the morning line to post. Positive means it shortened. This is descriptive only: because your payout is the final price regardless of when you bet, a horse that closes below our number was never a bet at our price — there is no earlier price to catch. A run of “short” closes is a sign our number was set against a loose morning line, not the real market.

Our call is an early hold/shorten read set from each flag’s committed board price — long-priced flags the crowd tends to leave alone hold, short ones get bet down. It is a heuristic on a small sample, shown next to what actually happened so you can watch it prove out or fail in public. Not a bet-timing tip: you are paid the closing price whenever you bet, so a flag we call “likely bet down” is simply a likely pass.
